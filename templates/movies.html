Movies and Short Films

“ Entertainment also helps.. "

Mental health can be a complex and sensitive subject to navigate, as it often involves personal struggles, societal pressures, and emotional challenges that are difficult to articulate. Yet, many films, short films, and YouTube videos offer insightful and moving portrayals of these issues, capturing the emotional depth and lived experiences of those dealing with mental health challenges. These creative works go beyond entertainment, helping to break the stigma around mental illness by fostering understanding, empathy, and open conversations.

From profound depictions of disorders like depression, anxiety, and schizophrenia to inspiring stories of resilience, recovery, and personal growth, these visual mediums provide valuable perspectives. They allow audiences to see themselves reflected in the characters, encouraging viewers to acknowledge their own struggles and realize they are not alone. Moreover, they serve as a source of education, helping to demystify mental health conditions by presenting accurate portrayals of psychological struggles, therapy, and healing.

Whether you're seeking guidance, reassurance, or simply a connection to a shared human experience, these films and videos can provide comfort and empowerment. They offer diverse approaches to mental health, from visualizing the inner workings of emotions to detailing the real-world effects of mental illness. In addition to offering relatable characters and stories, many also provide practical strategies for coping and managing mental health, showing how individuals can take small steps toward healing in their own lives.

These films, short films, and YouTube videos act as companions on the often-lonely journey toward mental well-being. They bring to light personal stories that inspire hope and change, showing us that recovery is possible, struggles are universal, and that seeking help is not only brave but essential. Below is a curated list of recommended content that can provide not only education but also the inspiration to continue moving forward, no matter the obstacles.

Good Mental Health and Wellbeing

The short film series “Short Films About Mental Health - Good Mental Health and Wellbeing” is designed to provide practical tips on maintaining mental health and wellbeing. These films, created with the involvement of young people who use mental health services, cover various aspects of mental health care, such as:

  • Getting Connected: Emphasizing the importance of social connections.
  • Getting Active: Highlighting physical activity’s role in mental health.
  • Sleeping Well: Offering tips for better sleep hygiene.
  • Eating Well: Discussing the impact of nutrition on mental health.
  • Being Mindful: Introducing mindfulness practices.
  • Tackling Stress: Providing strategies to manage stress.
  • Being Good at Something: Encouraging engagement in activities that build self-esteem.
  • Being Part of Something Bigger: Promoting involvement in community or group activities.

These films also acknowledge that while these practices can reduce the risk of mental health problems and help cope better, they do not guarantee immunity from mental health issues. They stress the importance of seeking help and support when needed.

We All Have Mental Health

The “We All Have Mental Health” animation is designed to help young people aged 11-14 understand mental health and how to take care of it. Created by the Anna Freud Centre, this animation provides a common language and understanding of mental health, showing examples of students dealing with overwhelming emotions and offering tips on how to manage them.

This animation is part of a broader toolkit aimed at Key Stage 3 students (Years 7-9), which includes lesson plans, assembly plans, and various classroom resources. These materials were developed in collaboration with young people, teachers, and mental health experts to ensure they are accessible and effective.

How Movies Help to Be Mentally Good?

Movies can have a profound impact on mental health and well-being in several ways:

Emotional Catharsis

  • Release of Emotions: Watching movies can provide a safe outlet for emotions. Whether it’s crying during a sad scene or laughing at a comedy, this emotional release can be therapeutic.

Empathy and Understanding

  • Building Empathy: Movies often tell stories from diverse perspectives, helping viewers understand and empathize with experiences different from their own. This can foster a sense of connection and reduce feelings of isolation.
  • Raising Awareness: Films about mental health issues can educate viewers, reduce stigma, and encourage conversations about mental health.

Inspiration and Motivation

  • Providing Hope: Many films tell inspiring stories of overcoming challenges, which can give viewers hope and motivation to address their own struggles.

Distraction and Relaxation

  • Providing a Break: Movies can offer a temporary escape from stress, providing relaxation and a chance to disconnect from daily worries.
